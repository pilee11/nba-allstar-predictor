Research Question

Can NBA All-Star selections be accurately predicted based on regular-season performance metrics alone — even though the process includes fan voting, player voting, and coaching discretion?

Two key challenges: subjective selection elements (reputation, market size, fan popularity) not captured by stats, and severe class imbalance with only ~5% of players selected annually.

Error Analysis
False Positives

Injury-related absences (Embiid, Harden) — model predicted All-Star due to high statistics, but they were absent in reality. Jokić/Dončić initially flagged due to name-mapping issues.

False Negatives

"Reputation Selections" (Bryant, Irving) — missed due to efficiency-focused modelling. Deni Avdija missed by just 0.06% confidence — fan voting pushed him over threshold in reality.

Engineered Features
1
Height-Conditional Metrics
Re-weights stats by position (interior vs. perimeter players), normalising by what's expected for each role.
2
Shooting Efficiency
Points per 36 min adjusted by FG%, with a 10 PPG floor. Penalises volume scorers with low efficiency.
3
Winning Impact
Individual ±Plus/Minus combined with team win percentage. Rewards players who win, not just score.
4
All-Star Momentum
Decay-weighted count of prior selections. Captures the "hype" effect — established stars get extra tailwind.

Spotlight — Deni Avdija

Israel's first NBA All-Star, Avdija was predicted as NOT an All-Star by the model — missing by a margin of just 0.06% confidence.

This is the clearest demonstration of the "human element" — nationwide fan campaigns and cultural momentum pushed him over the threshold in reality, something pure statistics cannot capture.

About This Project

A machine learning classification study investigating whether NBA All-Star selections can be accurately predicted from regular-season performance metrics. Built using 30 seasons of data (1996–2026) with a HistGradient Boosting classifier.

The project addresses two core challenges: subjective selection elements (fan voting, reputation) not captured by statistics, and severe class imbalance with only ~5% of players selected annually.

Methodology

1
Data Collection — NBA API + Basketball-Reference with manual injury & unicode corrections
2
EDA + Feature Engineering — 4 engineered features, VIF multicollinearity removal
3
Model Selection — 5 models benchmarked; HistGradient Boosting selected
4
4-Stage Optimisation — Sample weights → Regularisation → Threshold → Hyperparams
5
Blind Evaluation — 2025–26 season as unseen future test (AUC 0.987)
